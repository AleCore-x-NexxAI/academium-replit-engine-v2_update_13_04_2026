## Why: The Root Cause

The evidence points to one primary bottleneck: **your LLM provider can't handle this level of concurrent agent calls.** The symptoms are all consistent with this — rate limit hits, 5xx server errors, massive p95/max latency spikes, and error rates that scale up with VU count. The infrastructure layer is fine; the requests die once they reach the AI decision and reflection endpoints.

The secondary issue is that your agent error rate (83%) is far higher than your HTTP fail rate (52.5%), meaning many calls are technically completing but returning usable-but-bad responses — empty content, malformed JSON, or errors wrapped in 200s.

**Where to look next:**
- Check your LLM provider's rate limits and concurrency caps — you're likely hitting them hard at 50 concurrent students
- Consider adding a request queue or concurrency limiter on the agent side so you control the burst rather than the provider rejecting it
- Look into whether you're on a tier that supports higher TPM/RPM, or whether you need to add retry logic with backoff
- The 48 server errors at 50 VUs also suggests your own backend may be crashing or timing out when the agent responses are slow — worth checking your backend logs for that window
